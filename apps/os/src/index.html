SHAPE ROTATOR · cohort atlas

What you're looking at

Each colored region is a peer in your cohort — a country whose territory holds every page that peer has indexed. Your country sits at the center; friends radiate outward.

Every dot inside a country is a page that peer has fetched and offered to the cohort. Pages cluster by who fetched them, not by topic — so the wall reads as "this is what hyperion is into, this is what easel is into, this is what you're into."

When two peers index the same page, a faint caravan route arcs between their countries — co-contribution in the wild.

searxng-wth-frnds

Shape Rotator OS runs searxng-wth-frnds — a peer-to-peer meta search engine forked from SearXNG, built for collaborative searching. Every search you run is auto-shared with the cohort (scope: friends) and propagates over your LAN via mDNS.

no central server no telemetry nothing leaves the cohort unless you ask
